Scientists who study structural biology have access to a vast array of equipment to conduct their research, what is discussed is only a small sample fo the methods employed in the field on a routine basis

Catalysis

Enzymes lower the barriers necessary for chemical reactions by interacting with smaller molecules known as ligands, in a process known as catalysis

The quantitative study of this activity is known as kinetics, and the study of how this is exactly accomplished is known as mechanism

Imaging

Researchers desire to have atomic-resolution images of the compounds they study, and employ three main methods to accomplish this: Cryogenic Electron Microscopy, Homology Modeling, and X-Ray Crystallography

Spectroscopy

Researchers employ various additional methods to study biomolecule characteristics.

The use of concentrated radiation spectra, known as spectroscopy, allows a myriad of analytical methods to be employed, from determining protein concentration in solution, to detecting free radicals
