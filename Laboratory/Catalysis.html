What is Catalysis?

Catalysis is when an enzyme interacts with ligands. The exact process of how this is accomplished is known as mechanism

It is often misconstrued that the enzymes themselves conduct the reactions, which is false. Enzymes simply lower the amount of energy required to initiate the reaction

In order to gain insight into how efficiently an enzyme can process ligands, kinetic assayts are conducted.

Kinetics assays entail exposing enzymes to their ligands in a controlled environment, allowing the reaction to proceed, then evaluating the concentration of end product using UV-Vis spectroscopy.

Case Study: Michaelis Menten Kinetics Model

The Michaelis Menten Model (MM) is a mathematical model used to evaluate enzyme catalysis. MM takes a reductionist approach:

  • The enzyme reaction mechanism is instantaneous, with no delays or intermediates
  • The rate of binding between enzyme and ligand is equal to the rate of dissociation of enzyme and ligand, known as the Steady State Assumption

The MM model suggests that enzymes have a maximal velocity Vmax, in which regardless of ligand concentration, the enzyme can only react to a certain extent. This gives the MM model its distinct logarithmic curve, as shown below.
